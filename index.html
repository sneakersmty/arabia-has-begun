Turki Al-Sheikh: “Saudi Arabia has begun its journey towards attracting investors.”

October 31, 2019 0 By Leah Toms

Last October 17 was the start of the Riyadh Season festival. This event is said to create 46,000 seasonal jobs for the people of Saudi Arabia with 3,000 events and support services coming.

The chairman of the General Entertainment Authority and head of the Riyadh Season, Turki Al-Sheikh said, “There has been huge turnout of sponsors for the event. Around 80 percent of the target has been achieved with generating around SR400 million and this was unprecedented.”

Al-Sheikh also said at the opening session of the Joy Entertainment Industry Forum in Riyadh that the entertainment sector in the Kingdom set a goal of being able to produce 250,000 permanent jobs until 2030.

“There has been no withdrawal of organizers or participants of events thanks to the great confidence in the Kingdom’s economy,” he said at the opening of the event.

A series of discussions took place on the first day of the forum, including Minister of Economy and Planning Dr. Mohammed Al-Tuwaijri and Minister of Communications and Information Technology Abdullah Al-Sawaha.

He also noted that the events which recently just started in Saudi Arabia mark the beginning of investments in Saudi and from the private sector.

“Saudi Arabia has begun its journey towards attracting investors.”

“Saudi Arabia is in a global competition to attract the global investors, and there are many options for the investor and the consumer, as the tourism sector, including entertainment, represents 10 percent of the global economy and that the development of infrastructure, regulations, legislation, and incentives will work to attract investors in the tourism and entertainment sector,” said Al-Tuwaijri.

Minister of Communications and Information Technology, Abdullah Al-Sawaha said in his speech that The telecommunications industry generates nearly 4 percent of the gross domestic product (GDP). He also pointed out that, despite demand, the Saudi market is now ranked 19th in the world and the first in the Middle East.

“The gaming and digital content industry will change dramatically through augmented reality technologies,” the minister said.

The Forum witnessed the signing by GEA of a number of memoranda of understanding (MoU) with the Ministry of Communications and Information Technology, King Abdulaziz University (KAU) and the Kafala Program for the funding of Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs).

GEA Chairman Turki Al-Sheikh and Al-Sawaha signed the first MoU to establish human resources in the field of electronic games, in addition to fostering and supporting innovation in the electronic games market.

Another MOU included the exchange of skills in training and education with KAU and the third was concluded with the Kafala system and small and medium-sized companies operating in the entertainment field.

The Riyadh Forum is a new stage for a bright future in the Kingdom and is anticipated to have a profound impact on the local, international and regional levels through the large participation of companies and prominent global figures. In keeping with its traditions and customs, the Kingdom has become the perfect destination for entertainment.
